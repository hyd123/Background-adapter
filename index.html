Abstract

Image inpainting, which aims to restore the masked regions of an image, takes great advantage of the progress made in diffusion models (DMs). In the e-commerce field, image inpainting is widely applied in generating product images. Generally, an image featuring products and a clean background is provided, and the task is to generate a new image with the same products but a more suitable background. However, existing methods often struggle to endow the generated images with rich light, shadow, and texture. Therefore, we propose a novel and straightforward plugin architecture named the background-adapter for diffusion models, aiming to make the generated product images more vivid. Our experimental results demonstrate that the background-adapter outperforms existing models in several key metrics.
